What inspired us

Accesibility

Most testing platforms currently used by colleges put students with disabilities at a disadvantage. During in person examinations students with disabilities would be offered accommodations, to ensure that they can compete equally with their peers, such as scribes, exam papers in alternate formats and readers. These accommodations have not been implemented in online testing environments, making reading exams and answering the questions difficult during an already stressful period for these students. We wanted to create a platform that is customisable to a student's specific needs so that they aren't at a disadvantage.

Covid-19

Due to social distancing restrictions it is difficult for colleges and universities to hold their exams in person and they are instead turning to online testing platforms. The rush to move from in person exams to online exams meant that many students and teachers alike were left confused about online testing and how to use these platforms. As college students we felt like this was a big aspect of our lives and is something that has been effecting us since March for this reason we wanted to look at the issue and work on a solution.

Usability

As students we have used exam platforms and know that they don't always have an intuative design. Many exam platforms have too many features that can overwhelm users during an alreadying overwhelming period. We wanted our platform to to be simple and easy to use so that students and lecturers could quickly and easily do what they need to do.
